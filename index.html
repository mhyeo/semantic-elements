What are semantic elements?

Semantic elements are a way to organize our content to clearly communicate its purpose. But why should we use semantic elements? Non-semantic elements (such as div and span), give no information about the content within it beyond that different elements are separated. However, different semantic elements have different meanings and purpose. Thus, the browser will understand how to properly format the content, and other developers will know what the content's purpose is.

What kind of semantic elements are there?

Here are a few commonly used semantic elements:

header
For brief introductory or main information
footer
For minor details such as:
  • author
  • copyright information
  • contact informmation
main
For all main information
nav
For a group of links navigating to other related webpages
section
For organizing groups of information that makes sense by itself

But there are many more!
